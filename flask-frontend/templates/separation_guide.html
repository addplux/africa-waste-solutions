Separation at Source

Waste Separation Guide

Learn how to properly separate waste at the point of generation for effective recycling and environmental protection.

Why Separate Waste at Source?

Higher Quality Recyclables

Clean, separated materials fetch better prices and are easier to recycle

Reduced Contamination

Prevents mixing of hazardous and organic waste with recyclables

Environmental Protection

Reduces landfill waste and pollution in our communities

Waste Categories & How to Separate

Plastics

What to include:

  • Plastic bottles (water, soda, juice)
  • Plastic containers and packaging
  • Plastic bags (clean and dry)
  • Yogurt cups and food containers

⚠️ Important:

Rinse containers to remove food residue. Remove caps and labels when possible.

Glass

What to include:

  • Glass bottles (beer, wine, spirits)
  • Glass jars (jam, sauce, pickles)
  • Beverage bottles
  • Broken glass (wrap separately)

⚠️ Important:

Rinse bottles and jars. Separate by color if possible (clear, green, brown).

Paper & Cardboard

What to include:

  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Cardboard boxes (flattened)
  • Office paper and envelopes
  • Greasy pizza boxes

⚠️ Important:

Keep paper dry. Remove plastic windows from envelopes. Flatten boxes.

Metals

What to include:

  • Aluminum cans (soda, beer)
  • Steel/tin cans (food, pet food)
  • Metal bottle caps and lids
  • Aluminum foil (clean)

⚠️ Important:

Rinse cans thoroughly. Crush cans to save space. Remove labels if possible.

Organic Waste

What to include:

  • Fruit and vegetable scraps
  • Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • Eggshells and nutshells
  • Garden waste and leaves

⚠️ Important:

Keep separate from other waste. Can be composted at home or collected.

Hazardous Waste

What to include:

  • Batteries (all types)
  • Electronics and e-waste
  • Paint, chemicals, pesticides
  • Medical waste (needles, etc.)

⚠️ DANGER:

NEVER mix with regular waste. Contact us for special collection.

Step-by-Step Separation Process

1

Set Up Separation Bins

Have at least 3 bins at home: Recyclables (plastics, glass, paper, metal), Organic Waste, and General Waste. Label them clearly.

2

Rinse & Clean

Quickly rinse food containers, bottles, and cans with water. This prevents contamination and odors.

3

Remove Contaminants

Take off caps, lids, and labels when possible. Remove food residue from packaging.

4

Flatten & Compress

Flatten cardboard boxes and crush plastic bottles to save space in your bins.

5

Store Properly

Keep bins in a dry, accessible location. Empty regularly to prevent overflow and pests.

6

Schedule Collection

Use our platform to schedule pickup or drop off at designated collection points.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Bagging Recyclables in Plastic

Don't put recyclables in plastic bags. This contaminates the batch and makes sorting difficult.

Not Rinsing Containers

Food residue attracts pests and contaminates other recyclables, reducing their value.

Mixing Hazardous Waste

Never mix batteries, electronics, or chemicals with regular waste. These require special handling.

Wishful Recycling

Don't assume everything can be recycled. When in doubt, check our guide or contact us.
